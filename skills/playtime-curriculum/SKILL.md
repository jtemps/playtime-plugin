# Playtime curriculum reference

This skill maps a kid's age (in months under 3yr, in years over 3yr) to:

- **What they're working on** developmentally
- **What they typically can / can't do**
- **What activities will land** at that stage
- **What learning goals to nudge toward**

## How to use

When generating an activity for a kid:

1. Compute their age in months: `(target_date - birthday).days / 30.4`
2. Read the matching reference file:

3. Use the file's "what works" / "skip these" sections to filter activity choices.
4. Use the "developmental targets" section to weight learning goals.

## Domains

Every reference covers these domains so you can balance variety across days:

- **Cognitive** — problem-solving, attention, memory, reasoning
- **Language & literacy** — speech, vocabulary, reading, writing
- **Math & numeracy** — counting, quantity, patterns, operations
- **Fine motor** — hand control, scissors, drawing, writing
- **Gross motor** — whole-body coordination, balance
- **Social-emotional** — feelings, regulation, relationships, identity
- **Creative arts** — music, visual art, dramatic play, dance
- **Science & STEM** — observation, hypothesis, classification
- **Practical life** — self-care, household tasks, real tools
- **Cultural / world awareness** — geography, cultures, history (older kids)

A good week of packets touches several of these. A great month touches all of them.

## Important notes

- **Every kid is different.** These bands are typical, not prescriptive. If the parent's notes say "advanced for age" or "delayed in X", weight to that, not the chart.
- **Don't pathologize variance.** Materials should celebrate where the kid IS, not chase milestones.
- **Months matter under 3.** A 16-month-old and a 23-month-old are different humans. Don't lump them.
- **Years matter under 6.** Bigger gaps OK after that.
- **Sensory and emotional load grows non-linearly.** A 4yr-old can handle scary themes a 3yr-old cannot.
