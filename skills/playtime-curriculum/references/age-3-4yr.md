# Age 3-4 years

Storytellers, pretenders, askers-of-a-thousand-questions. Magic age for narrative play. Pre-academic skills emerging — letter/number recognition, pre-writing strokes, counting with meaning. Friendship begins. Fears do too.

## What's happening developmentally

- 1000-2000+ word vocabulary; 4-6 word sentences; uses past tense (often hilariously: "I goed")
- Asks "why?" — the famous phase. Genuine curiosity, not provocation
- Recognizes some letters (often own initials first); names some numbers; counts 1-10 with one-to-one correspondence to ~5
- Draws a circle, vertical and horizontal lines; person = circle with arms/legs ("tadpole person")
- Cuts with kid scissors (with help); strings large beads; uses tripod grasp emerging
- Hops on one foot; pedals confidently; throws a ball with intent
- Pretend play is rich and sustained — can play one scenario for 30+ minutes
- Theory of mind firms up — understands others can be mistaken
- Fears emerge: dark, monsters, separation, doctors

## What works

- **Stories** — short illustrated stories the parent reads aloud, with a question or two for the kid to answer
- **Mad libs (very simple)** — 2-3 blanks, all picture-prompted ("a [picture: animal]" with options to circle)
- **Connect-the-dots 1-10 or 1-15** — clear path, no crossing
- **Mazes** — single-path, 6-10 turns, narrow enough to be a challenge
- **Coloring pages** — bigger sections, can introduce some detail
- **Drawing prompts with structure** — "finish the picture" (we draw the head, they draw the body), step-by-step "how to draw a fish" with 4 simple steps
- **Letter and number tracing** — single letter or single number per page, big and dotted
- **Counting activities** — count-and-color, count-and-circle, simple addition with pictures (1+1, 2+1)
- **Pattern completion** — AB, AAB, ABC patterns
- **Spot the difference** — 3-5 differences, big and obvious
- **Matching games** — match by category, by sound (rhymes), by attribute
- **I-spy** — picture-based, rich illustration with hidden items
- **Crafts** — multi-step but short: paper plate animals, handprint art, cotton ball clouds, paper bag puppets
- **Science experiments** — color mixing, sink/float, magnet hunt, leaf rubbing
- **Cooking** — stir, sprinkle, pour, decorate. Simple recipes with pictures
- **Scavenger hunts** — picture list + word, indoor or outdoor
- **Pretend play scenarios** — printable cards: vet clinic, restaurant, post office
- **Yoga / movement** — animal pose cards, freeze dance, follow-the-leader
- **Acts of kindness** — daily prompts ("today, draw a picture for someone you love")
- **Feelings activities** — emotion wheels (4-6 feelings), "how does the bunny feel?" picture prompts
- **Concept-of-the-day** — opposites, big/small, fast/slow, full/empty
- **Songs, finger plays, rhymes**

## Skip these

- Crosswords, word searches with letters
- Sudoku, logic puzzles
- Long chapter stories
- Anything assuming reading
- Strategy games
- Multi-page activities

## Print packet style

- One main activity centerpiece (half the page)
- One small "extra" (joke, riddle, single I-spy moment, "today's word")
- Greeting with kid's name + day/weather
- Grown-up footer: prep + ~1 line on what it's teaching + extension idea
- Easy-to-read serif/handwriting-style font for any text the kid will see

## Frameworks fit

- **Good Inside**: emotion-coaching scripts everywhere, especially around fears
- **Hunt Gather Parent**: alloparenting, cooperative tasks, "we" before "I"
- **Bringing Up Bébé**: "le cadre" — clear frame with autonomy inside; food culture is age-relevant (cooking)
- **Reggio Emilia**: project-based — same theme across a week, with daily evolution
- **Montessori**: 3-period lessons, control of error, beautiful materials
- **Whole-Brain Child**: name-it-to-tame-it; SIFT (sensations, images, feelings, thoughts) for older end of this band
- **How To Talk So Little Kids Will Listen**: communication scripts in parent footer

## Learning goal nudges

- Letter recognition (start with name letters)
- Counting to 20 rote; 1:1 to 10
- Drawing recognizable people (4+ body parts)
- Cutting along a line
- Tripod pencil grasp
- Identifying basic shapes (circle, square, triangle)
- Naming all primary + secondary colors
- Following 3-step instructions
- Answering "what" and "where" questions; starting "why"
- Telling simple stories from a picture

## Sensitivities watch

- Fears: dark, monsters, separation, doctors, big animals — check sensitivities before themes like "monsters" or "predators"
- Win/lose: still tender; design cooperative
- Failure: still devastating; every answer is celebrated
- Bathroom humor: starts now; mostly fine; just know it'll appear in their mad-lib answers

## Common obsessions to ride

Dinosaurs · construction vehicles · princesses · superheroes · ninjas · dragons · unicorns · the ocean · space · trains · firefighting · doctors · pets · bugs · "why" everything
