# Age 6-12 months

The "exploration explosion" period. Sitting → crawling → cruising → maybe first steps. Object permanence is forming. Babbling becomes proto-words. This is where the packet starts to feel like a real activity — but still **caregiver-led, baby-driven**.

## What's happening developmentally

- **6-7mo**: sitting unsupported, reaching across midline, mouthing everything (this is *learning*, not bad behavior)
- **7-9mo**: pincer grasp emerging, transferring objects, banging two objects together, peekaboo lights up
- **8-10mo**: object permanence solidifies (peekaboo finally HITS), stranger awareness, separation anxiety can spike
- **9-11mo**: pulling to stand, cruising along furniture, pointing, "mama"/"dada" with meaning
- **10-12mo**: first steps for some, first true words, container play (in/out is a religion), waving, clapping
- Cause and effect is the big cognitive theme — **drop = noise = repeat**
- Receptive language is way ahead of expressive — they understand much more than they say

## What works

- **Object permanence games** — printed scenes with peek-a-boo flaps (the parent cuts and folds), or a simple "find the duck" hide-and-seek script
- **Peekaboo scripts and variations** — under a scarf, behind hands, behind a board book
- **Container play prompts** — "today's invitation: a basket, three cups, two balls, and a wooden spoon"
- **Treasure baskets** — printed shopping/gathering list of household objects with varied texture/weight (Montessori roots)
- **Cause-and-effect set-ups** — push-ball ramp from a cookbook stand and a tube; rattle-shaker recipes
- **Songs with hand gestures** — Wheels on the Bus, If You're Happy
- **Mirror exploration** scripts
- **First-word prompting** — printable picture cards (single high-contrast objects)
- **Sensory bins** — supervised, large items only (NO small parts)
- **Stand-and-cruise prompts** — set up a "trail" along the couch with sticky tape targets

## Skip these

- Anything with small parts (under-3 choking rule, but especially here)
- Crayons/markers (a few will, most won't yet — and they'll eat them)
- Scissors, glue, paint
- Stories longer than 1-2 minutes
- Anything requiring sustained sitting >5min
- Mazes, mad libs, puzzles (other than 1-2 piece chunky knob puzzles)

## Print packet style for 6-12mo

Still parent-facing, but warmer + more interactive:

- One main "invitation" (a setup the parent prepares — takes 60 seconds)
- A song or rhyme to sing during the activity
- 1-2 alternative invitations for if the main one flops
- A "what to watch for" — the developmental win to celebrate this week
- A printable picture card or two (single object, big, high-contrast) for naming

## Frameworks fit

- **RIE**: still a perfect fit — observation, slow care, narrating, uninterrupted floor time
- **Montessori**: treasure baskets, low shelves with 3-4 toys, real objects over plastic
- **Waldorf**: natural-fiber play silks, wooden objects, gentle rhythm
- **Good Inside / Dr. Becky**: emotional attunement scripts for stranger anxiety, separation moments
- **Hunt Gather Parent**: alloparenting prompts, "let them help" minute (mash a banana together)

## Learning goal nudges

- Sitting, then cruising, then walking
- Pincer grasp (offer puffs, peas, blueberries)
- First word with meaning
- Pointing to a desired object
- Waving / clapping on cue
- Following a simple instruction ("can you give me the ball?")
- Object permanence — peekaboo joy

## Sensitivities watch

- Stranger anxiety / separation peak around 8-10mo — packets that involve OTHER people should be optional
- Loud sounds — keep music gentle
