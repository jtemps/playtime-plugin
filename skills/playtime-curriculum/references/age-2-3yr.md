# Age 2-3 years (24-36 months)

Imagination + storytelling + the climb toward independence. "I do it myself" energy. Pretend play has plot. Vocabulary explodes (200 → 1000+ words by 3). Big feelings + still-tiny coping. Half the day is a victory; half the day is a meltdown. Both are normal.

## What's happening developmentally

- **24-30mo**: 200-500 words; 3-word sentences; rote-counts to 3-5; recognizes a few colors; knows own name; runs and jumps; turns single book pages; circles emerging in scribbles
- **30-36mo**: 500-1000+ words; full sentences; understands "in/on/under"; rote-counts to 10; matches colors; pedals tricycle; draws a circle; copies vertical line; some kids start naming letters; pretend play has plot ("the bear is sick, we need to take her to the doctor")
- Theory of mind is forming — start to understand others have different feelings/thoughts
- Empathy emerges — they may comfort a crying friend
- Self-regulation is *just starting* — needs heavy co-regulation
- Schemas: still going strong — connection, orientation, enveloping (wrapping, hiding inside)

## What works

- **Story prompts and pretend play scenarios** — printable "today's story": setting + characters + a problem to solve through play
- **Drawing prompts** — "draw a face!" "make rain", "what does Mommy look like?" — celebrate scribble-attempts
- **Counting activities** — count-and-color (1-5 or 1-10), counting hunts ("find 4 leaves outside")
- **Pre-tracing** — big curves, lines, dots-to-line. Not letters yet for most
- **Stickers as currency** — a sheet of stickers + outlines to fill. HUGE win.
- **Big-shape coloring** — large simple shapes, no fine detail. One color goal: "make this fish all yellow"
- **Pattern start** — "what comes next? red, blue, red, blue, ___" with cut-out blocks or stickers
- **Matching pages** — match the animal to its baby; match the picture to the picture
- **Simple connect-the-dots** — 1-5, big dots, big spacing
- **First mazes** — 4-6 turns, very wide path, single solution
- **Scavenger hunts** — picture-based ("find these 5 things in the house") — they cross them off
- **Sensory bins** with themes — rescue dinosaurs from the "tar pit" (oats), excavate ice cubes
- **Yoga / animal poses** — printable cards with cartoon kid in pose
- **First science** — observation: "what floats? what sinks?", color mixing with water + food coloring
- **Cooking** — with HEAVY help: stirring, sprinkling, peeling clementines, washing veggies
- **Acts-of-kindness prompts** — single-step ("draw something for a grown-up")
- **Feelings check-in** — printable face cards (happy, sad, mad, scared) — point to how you feel
- **Songs and dances** — Five Little Ducks, Tooty Ta, freeze dance
- **First storytelling** — picture sequence (3 pictures, tell what happens)

## Skip these

- Mad libs — don't yet have parts of speech
- Word search, crossword, sudoku — too abstract
- Detailed coloring pages — fine motor isn't there
- Logic puzzles
- Long stories with chapters
- Strategy games
- Activities with rules that have to be followed
- Anything where losing is part of the design

## Print packet style

- ONE main activity per page, with personality
- Big visuals, low word density
- Optional "for grown-ups" footer with prep + extension ideas
- A small extra: a single joke ("what does a cat say? meow!"), a tiny finger play, or a one-line "today's word" with a picture
- Use the kid's name in titles and instructions

## Frameworks fit

- **Good Inside**: peak relevance — this is the age of meltdowns. Print emotion-coaching scripts in the parent footer
- **Montessori**: practical life expands (pour from real pitcher, button practice frame, sweep), 3-period lesson for new vocabulary
- **Hunt Gather Parent**: TEAM is fully online — autonomy + togetherness + minimal interference
- **RIE / Magda Gerber**: continues to support uninterrupted play, observation
- **Reggio Emilia**: long projects start to become possible — ride an obsession across a week
- **Whole-Brain Child**: name-it-to-tame-it scripts for emotion moments

## Learning goal nudges

- Following 2-3 step instructions
- Counting to 10 rote, with 1:1 correspondence to 5
- Naming 4-6 colors
- Drawing a circle
- Identifying own name in print (some)
- Sorting by 1-2 attributes
- Pretend play sequences with plot
- First letters (some kids; do not push)
- Toilet learning (often happening; not curriculum)

## Sensitivities watch

- Scary themes (witches, monsters, large predators) — check sensitivities; some kids LOVE, some are deeply unsettled
- Loud sounds in audio prompts
- Failure framing — at this age "you got it wrong" is devastating; design for *every* answer to be celebrated
- Transition warnings needed
- Win/lose game design — avoid; favor cooperative

## Common obsessions to ride

Dinosaurs · vehicles (esp. trash trucks, fire trucks, construction) · superheroes · princesses · dogs/cats · ocean animals · trains · weather · the moon · grown-up roles (doctor, chef, builder)
