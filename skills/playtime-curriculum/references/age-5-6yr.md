# Age 5-6 years

Kindergarten energy. Reading takes off (some are fluent, some are sounding out, some are pre-readers — wide range is normal). Writing emerges. Friendships and rules dominate social life. Big imaginative play and pretend continue but get more rule-bound.

## What's happening developmentally

- Decoding CVC words → simple sentences; some kids reading independently
- Writing letters and short words; some kids writing sentences
- Counting to 100; simple addition/subtraction within 10 (some within 20); skip-counting by 2s, 5s, 10s
- Drawings have detail: sky, ground, sun, recognizable house, person with clothing
- Cuts shapes accurately; ties shoes (some); rides a bike
- Theory of mind well-established; understands jokes, double meanings emerging
- Cooperative play; rules-based games; tattling phase
- Self-regulation improving — but still needs co-regulation under stress

## What works

- **Stories** — read-aloud with longer plots, multiple characters; some early readers can handle short illustrated chapter books
- **Mad libs** — 6-10 blanks, real parts of speech (noun, verb, adjective)
- **Choose-your-own-adventure** — 4-6 decision points, branching plots
- **Connect-the-dots 1-50 or letter-sequence (alphabet path)**
- **Mazes** — complex, multi-path, with mini-puzzles to solve at checkpoints
- **Coloring with fine detail** — mandalas at child level, intricate scenes
- **Drawing prompts** — open-ended: "design a city of the future", "what's the dragon's secret?"
- **Step-by-step how-to-draw** — more complex (8-10 steps)
- **Spot the difference** — 10-15 differences, more subtle
- **Crosswords (kid-level)** — picture clues, 6-10 entries
- **Word searches** — real text, theme-related
- **Sudoku** — 4x4 picture sudoku
- **Logic puzzles** — sequences, sorting, simple deductive ("which one is mine?")
- **Rebus puzzles** — picture-word combinations
- **Cipher / secret code** — substitute letters, decode a message
- **Math puzzles** — number lines, simple word problems with pictures, 2-digit arithmetic with pictures
- **Crafts** — multi-day projects, weaving, paper folding
- **Science experiments** — slightly more complex: density tower, dancing raisins, tornado in a bottle, solar prints, simple circuits with playdough + LED
- **Cooking** — full simple recipes with adult supervision (pancakes, muffins, no-knead bread, smoothies)
- **Scavenger hunts** — text-based clues, treasure-hunt format with map
- **Origami** — 6-8 fold projects (crane, frog, jumping animal)
- **Magic tricks** — card tricks, vanish-the-coin, prediction tricks
- **Comic strip creation** — 4-6 panels with speech bubbles
- **Map-making** — neighborhood map, treasure map
- **Time capsule** — formal: handprint, height, favorites, drawing of self
- **Family interview** — multi-question with prompts, recorded as kid writes/draws
- **Concept of the day** — virtues (perseverance, honesty, generosity), with stories
- **Letter writing** — to a grandparent, a future-self, a stuffie
- **Riddles, knock-knock jokes, brain teasers**
- **Pattern + symmetry** — drawing the other half, mirroring designs
- **Pretend play kits** — extended printable scenarios with character roles, dialogue starters

## Skip these

- High-text content with no pictures
- Long-form math drills
- Complex strategy games
- Anything assuming pre-algebra

## Print packet style

- Reading scaffolded — text in chunks, pictures, phonetic spellings for tricky words if useful
- Kid-facing instructions can be longer
- Multi-step activities OK
- Greeting + day vibe + activity title

## Frameworks fit

- **Hunt Gather Parent**: contribution-as-identity, "you're a helper in this family"
- **Bringing Up Bébé**: independence + culture-of-patience
- **Reggio Emilia**: deep projects, hundred languages, peer collaboration
- **Montessori**: cosmic education starts (timeline of life, geography), elementary materials
- **Positive Discipline**: family meetings start to work
- **Ross Greene**: collaborative problem-solving conversations
- **Whole-Brain Child**: integration, narrating the past
- **Simplicity Parenting**: rhythm + less stuff still relevant

## Learning goal nudges

- Read CVC words; some sight words; some short sentences
- Write own name + simple words; some short sentences
- Count to 100; identify 2-digit numbers
- Add/subtract within 10; skip-count by 2s/5s/10s
- Tell a 3-part story with detail
- Tie shoes
- Ride a bike
- Cooperative play with peers
- Show empathy unprompted
- Begin to handle losing in a game (still hard)

## Sensitivities watch

- Fairness obsession: design cooperatively
- Failure: better but still tender
- Heavy themes (loss, illness): handle with care; consult `sensitivities`
- Peer comparison emerging: avoid framing that makes them feel "behind"

## Common obsessions to ride

Pokémon · superheroes · LEGO · video games (often) · Minecraft · mythology · sports (some) · dance · specific shows · drawing · jokes
