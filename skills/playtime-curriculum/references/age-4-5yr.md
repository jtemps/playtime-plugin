# Age 4-5 years

Pre-K powerhouse. Pre-readers becoming readers. Drawings get detailed. Friendships matter. Big imagination, big questions ("what's death?"), big feelings still. Rules of games start to make sense — but they can also make their own up mid-game.

## What's happening developmentally

- 2000-2500+ word vocabulary; complex sentences; storytelling has structure (beginning/middle/end, even if rambling)
- Recognizes most letters; many can write own name; rote-counts to 30+; 1:1 correspondence to 15-20; understands "more than/less than"
- Pre-reading: phonological awareness — rhyming, syllables, first letter sounds (some kids start sounding out CVC words)
- Drawings: people with face features, body parts, often clothing; can copy a square, triangle; draws a recognizable person
- Cuts on a line; cuts simple shapes; uses tripod grasp; handles glue stick
- Hops, gallops, skips (many); rides a balance bike or pedal bike with training wheels
- Theory of mind solid — can deceive (white lie), keep a secret, predict what others think
- Friendships have favorites; fairness matters intensely
- Existential questions land: death, where babies come from, why do bad things happen

## What works

- **Stories with chapters or scenes** — read-aloud with comprehension questions
- **Mad libs** — 4-6 blanks with simple parts of speech (animal, color, place, action) — picture-aided word banks for non-readers
- **Choose-your-own-adventure** — short, 2-3 decision points, big picture choices
- **Connect-the-dots 1-30** — themed (the kid sees the picture emerge)
- **Mazes** — multi-path, dead ends, 12-20 turns
- **Coloring with detail** — defined sections, multi-color
- **Drawing prompts** — "draw your superpower", "design a spaceship", "what's in the suitcase?"
- **Step-by-step how-to-draw** — 6-step illustrated guides for animals, vehicles, characters
- **Spot the difference** — 6-10 differences
- **I-spy** — busy scenes, find 12-15 items
- **Word search (with picture key)** — find the picture-words in a small grid
- **Sight word activities** — find/circle/highlight (the, and, is, mom, dad, name)
- **Letter sound activities** — "circle all the things that start with B" with pictures
- **Number activities** — counting 1-30, simple addition/subtraction within 10 with pictures, more/less
- **Pattern recognition** — AABB, ABCD patterns
- **Logic puzzles** — simple sorting/sequencing, 3-step "what comes next"
- **Crafts** — 4-6 step crafts: paper bag puppets with details, paper plate masks, weaving with strips
- **Science experiments** — density columns (oil/water), invisible ink (lemon juice), bouncy egg, leaf vein rubbings, simple chromatography
- **Cooking** — measure, pour, mix, decorate; can help follow a 4-step recipe with pictures
- **Scavenger hunts** — text + picture lists, can be longer
- **Pretend play** — extended scenarios; printable "play kits" with character cards
- **Origami** — 3-5 fold projects (boat, hat, dog face)
- **Magic tricks** — simple sleight-of-hand they can perform for a grown-up
- **Yoga + movement cards**
- **Comic strip making** — 3-panel template, kid fills in
- **Concept of the day** — empathy, courage, fairness, gratitude — story + question
- **Acts of kindness** — small, doable, weekly track
- **Time capsule prompts** — "today I am ___ tall, my favorite food is ___"
- **Family interview prompts** — kid asks Grandma or Dad questions and records answers (drawings ok)
- **Map-making** — draw the route from bedroom to kitchen
- **Riddles + jokes** — kid-friendly, with a punch line they'll get
- **Cipher (introductory)** — picture-substitution code, decode a sentence

## Skip these

- Crosswords (most still hard)
- Sudoku
- Heavy text-only stories
- Strategy games requiring planning > 2 moves
- Sarcasm or irony in writing

## Print packet style

- Mix of "do it yourself" + "ask a grown-up to read"
- Reading-level guidance: short, simple words; lots of pictures
- Header includes a personalized greeting + day's vibe
- Grown-up footer: prep + learning goals hit + extension
- Optional "show off" panel — a place to write/draw the kid's response

## Frameworks fit

- **Good Inside**: still highly relevant — fears + identity questions
- **Hunt Gather Parent**: cooperation, contribution to family ("you're our chief plant-waterer this week")
- **Bringing Up Bébé**: independence, food adventurousness, patience-building
- **Reggio Emilia**: project-based — themes can run for a full week with deepening complexity
- **Montessori**: practical life expands, sensorial work, intro to geography/zoology
- **Positive Discipline**: encouragement language, problem-solving routines
- **Ross Greene (CPS)**: solving lagging skills collaboratively
- **Whole-Brain Child**: SIFT, name-it-to-tame-it, integration
- **Simplicity Parenting**: rhythm, less stuff, deeper play

## Learning goal nudges

- Recognize all letters; know most letter sounds
- Write own name unprompted
- Count to 50; 1:1 to 20
- Add and subtract within 5 (with fingers/objects)
- Read 5-10 sight words
- Cut on a curve
- Tie shoes (advanced for this age, normal for next)
- Tell a 3-part story
- Identify own and others' feelings
- Take turns with a friend

## Sensitivities watch

- Fears expand: imaginary harm (kidnapping, fire, monster under bed) — be thoughtful with story themes
- Existential questions: respect them; we're not avoiding them but not introducing them
- Fairness: design for it ("Everyone gets a turn")
- Failure: starting to handle it better but still a tender spot
