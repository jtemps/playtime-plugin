# Age 6-8 years

Early elementary. Reading consolidates and accelerates. Writing becomes a real medium. Math gets formal. Independent project work is possible. Collections, hobbies, tribes, deep interests. The "industry vs inferiority" stage — confidence comes from competence.

## What's happening developmentally

- Most kids reading independently by 7; chapter books accessible (Junie B Jones, Magic Tree House, Roald Dahl)
- Writing sentences, then paragraphs; spelling improving (still wonderfully phonetic)
- Math: addition/subtraction within 100, intro multiplication, place value, fractions concept
- Drawings: scenes with foreground/background, attempts at depth
- Fine motor: cursive (some schools), tying complex knots, small construction
- Logical thinking developing — concrete operations (Piaget) — they handle conservation, classification, seriation
- Best friends; "groups"; gender separation common; rule-following intense
- Self-regulation noticeably better; can hold a project across days
- Sense of fairness and morality strong
- Humor: puns, knock-knocks, gross humor (poop jokes)

## What works

- **Chapter book read-alongs** — episodic stories, comprehension questions
- **Mad libs** — full grammatical (noun/verb/adjective/adverb), longer texts (100+ words)
- **Choose-your-own-adventure** — 8-12 decision points
- **Connect-the-dots** — alphabet, double-letter (A1-A26 then B1-B26), high-count (1-100)
- **Complex mazes** — multi-page, with story integration
- **Word puzzles** — word searches, mini-crosswords, anagrams, hangman printables
- **Sudoku** — 4x4 number, intro to 6x6
- **Cipher / secret message** — Caesar cipher, pigpen
- **Logic puzzles** — Einstein-style (5 clues to deduce who owns what), grid logic
- **Math puzzles** — magic squares, number snake, KenKen-light, multiplication hunts
- **Drawing prompts** — open-ended, story-driven; how-to-draw (10-15 step guides)
- **Comic strip + zine creation** — multi-page, full storytelling
- **Crafts** — multi-day, more complex: paper mache, friendship bracelets, simple sewing, weaving, beading
- **Science experiments** — predictions, observations, write-ups: oobleck, crystal growing, plant transpiration, simple machines
- **Cooking** — kid handles most of recipe with adult nearby; bakes from scratch (cookies, breads)
- **Scavenger hunts** — multi-clue, map-based, indoor/outdoor
- **Origami** — complex (10-15 fold) animals, modular origami
- **Magic tricks** — full routines, prop-making
- **Maps and geography** — country naming, capital cities, continents, "where in the world?" challenges
- **Time + measurement** — clock-reading practice (analog), measuring ingredients, weighing
- **Money** — counting coins, simple budgeting games
- **Letter writing** — pen pal, thank-yous, future-self letters
- **Journaling prompts** — daily one-liner; gratitude, highlight, lowlight
- **Family interviews** — themed: "tell me about when you were little"
- **Concept of the day** — virtues, history, science concepts, vocabulary
- **Word of the day** — with definition, example sentence, "use it 3 times today" challenge
- **Pattern and symmetry** — symmetry drawing, tessellations
- **Building challenges** — LEGO with constraints, cardboard engineering, paper engineering
- **Spy / detective games** — fingerprint, code-breaking, suspect-deduction
- **Riddles + brain teasers** — lateral thinking, classic kid riddles
- **Acts of kindness** — weekly track, with reflection
- **Goal-setting prompts** — what do you want to learn this month?
- **Research mini-project** — pick an animal/place/topic, fill in a printable "research page"

## Skip these

- Long workbook drills
- Strategy games beyond their attention
- Heavy abstract math
- Pure-text content with no visuals (works for some kids; not all)

## Print packet style

- More text OK; still illustrated
- Kid does the activity mostly independently
- Parent footer is for adult enrichment, not for handholding
- Multi-page allowed for projects

## Frameworks fit

- **Hunt Gather Parent**: contribution + apprenticeship — real tasks, real tools
- **Bringing Up Bébé**: autonomy expanding; "le cadre" still meaningful
- **Reggio Emilia**: long projects, "hundred languages", documentation
- **Montessori**: cosmic education in full swing — Great Lessons, timeline of humans, geography
- **Positive Discipline**: family meetings, encouragement, problem-solving
- **Ross Greene**: collaborative problem-solving for lagging skills
- **Whole-Brain Child**: integration, narrating, mindsight
- **Simplicity Parenting**: rhythm, less, slower

## Learning goal nudges

- Reading independently
- Writing paragraphs
- Multiplication facts (start)
- Telling time (analog)
- Counting money
- Persistent interests pursued (collections, hobbies)
- Taking on a household contribution
- Solving a problem with a friend without adult help

## Sensitivities watch

- Loss themes: handle with care
- Peer comparison: real now; design for personal mastery, not ranking
- "Hard" framing: kid may say "this is for babies" or "this is too easy" — adjust quickly

## Common obsessions to ride

Pokémon · Minecraft · LEGO · sports · dance · specific YouTubers/influencers (handle thoughtfully) · video games · graphic novels · animals · dinosaurs · space · mythology (Greek, Norse) · history (ancient Egypt, knights) · mystery/detective · jokes
