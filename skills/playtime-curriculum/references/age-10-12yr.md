# Age 10-12 years

Tween / preadolescent. Reading + abstract thinking solid. Hormones starting (varies wildly). Identity work intensifies. Friendships are everything; family less central. Humor is sophisticated. Don't condescend. They smell it.

## What's happening developmentally

- Abstract thought + hypothetical reasoning fully online
- Reading: novels, nonfiction, opinion pieces; some reading at adult level
- Writing: essays with arguments, long creative pieces
- Math: pre-algebra, algebra intro, geometry, probability
- Identity: "who am I, who are my people, what do I stand for" — actively constructing
- Social: belonging is central; friendship rifts are huge events
- Emotional: capacity for nuance + empathy + self-reflection growing; can also be sullen
- Self-regulation: variable; hormones complicate; sleep is undermined by school + screens
- Humor: irony, sarcasm, absurdism, deadpan

## What works

- **Long-form story writing prompts** — character sheets, plot frameworks, "write the next chapter"
- **Mad libs** — dialed-up; satire, parody, current-events flavored
- **Choose-your-own-adventure** — complex branching, moral choices
- **Crosswords + word puzzles** — adult-difficulty kid-themed
- **Cryptograms / ciphers** — multi-layer, real cryptography intro (substitution, transposition, simple frequency analysis)
- **Sudoku** — 9x9 standard
- **Logic grids** — challenging
- **Strategy puzzles** — chess puzzles, Go puzzles, set theory intros
- **Math** — algebra puzzles, real-world math (compound interest, statistics on something they care about)
- **Science** — proper experiments + write-ups; lab notebook style
- **Coding** — Python paper challenges, real coding projects via Scratch/Replit
- **Engineering** — Rube Goldberg machines, electronics with breadboards, robotics
- **Crafts** — sewing real things, leather, woodworking with adult supervision, real cooking
- **Detective + escape room packets** — full mystery, multi-session
- **Research projects** — proper sources, citations, deep dive on an interest
- **Op-ed / persuasive writing** — pick a stance, defend it
- **Poetry** — sonnets, slam poetry, song lyrics
- **Comics + zines** — full graphic novels in miniature
- **Graphic design challenges** — logo for a band, cover for a book, magazine spread
- **Music** — composition prompts (pick 4 chords, write a melody), lyric writing
- **Photography prompts** — daily themes
- **Filmmaking storyboards** — short film outlines
- **Geography + history** — current events kid-versions, geopolitics 101, history deep dives
- **Philosophy lite** — Trolley Problem, thought experiments, ethical dilemmas
- **Personal finance** — budgeting, saving, real-world money math
- **Goal setting + reflection** — vision board, monthly review
- **Volunteering / community impact** — plan + execute
- **Mindfulness + journaling** — gratitude, big-feelings journaling, body-scan prompts
- **Family + friendship reflections** — what kind of friend am I? what does my family value?
- **Ad-libs of irony** — mad libs that are arch, not childish
- **Concept-of-the-day** — vocabulary, history, philosophy, science deep cuts

## Skip these

- Babyish illustrations
- Forced "fun"
- Anything condescending

## Print packet style

- Full-text comfort; clean layout
- Smart, sometimes wry tone
- Long-form possible
- Less parent footer; kid is fully autonomous

## Frameworks fit

- **Bringing Up Bébé**: independence respected
- **Hunt Gather Parent**: meaningful contribution + apprenticeship
- **Ross Greene**: collaborative problem-solving on real issues (screens, sleep, friends)
- **Positive Discipline**: family meetings = real
- **Whole-Brain Child**: mindsight, narrating own story
- **How To Talk So Teens Will Listen**: communication scripts for parents
- **Free-range parenting**: real responsibility, real-world skills

## Learning goal nudges

- Critical reading
- Persuasive writing
- Pre-algebra → algebra
- Self-directed learning on real interests
- Healthy friendship maintenance
- Beginning of self-advocacy
- Real-world skills (cooking a meal, doing laundry, riding transit, basic finance)
- Identity work — knowing what they like, value, stand for

## Sensitivities watch

- Body image, appearance, weight: NEVER frame around these
- Identity (gender, sexuality, neurodiversity, race, religion): be thoughtful and inclusive
- Friend drama: validate but don't lead
- Mental health: depression and anxiety can present here; if generative content surfaces concerns, parent footer should gently note resources
- Don't condescend. Ever.

## Common obsessions to ride

Whatever they're into — and listen to *them*, not the chart. Common: gaming · streamers/YouTubers · K-pop · anime · sports · dance · drawing · skateboarding · D&D · card games · specific book series · social justice topics · animals · weird science · history · their friend group · music
