# Age 18-24 months

Language explosion + parallel play + the genesis of "no". 50-200+ word vocabulary for many. Two-word phrases. Pretend play takes off. Schemas still rule. They want to DO what grown-ups do — practical life is gold.

## What's happening developmentally

- **18-20mo**: 20-50 words → 100+ words; "more please", "all done"; identifies common objects in pictures; runs (clumsily); jumps in place
- **20-22mo**: two-word phrases regularly; sorts by one attribute; turns book pages; uses spoon and fork; stacks 4-6 blocks
- **22-24mo**: 200+ words for many; three-word phrases; pretend play has scenes (cooking + serving + eating); points to named body parts; first colors
- Pretend play with sequence (give doll a bath, then put doll to bed)
- Schemas continue (transporting, enclosing, rotation, trajectory) plus connection (linking, taping, stringing) and orientation (looking at things from new angles)
- Parallel play — playing alongside, not with, other kids
- Emotions are intense; co-regulation is key

## What works

- **Pretend play scenarios** — printable "menu" for a play-restaurant, list of doll-care steps, vet check-up cards
- **Practical life prompts** — water the plants, "wash" plastic dishes in the sink, peel a clementine, wipe the table
- **Color matching** — print a sheet with 3-4 big color blobs; find objects in the house that match
- **Counting routines** — count steps up the stairs, count blueberries, count fingers
- **Simple stickers + outline** — peel and place stickers inside a big shape
- **Big-paper scribbling** — directional prompts: "make rain coming down", "draw the storm"
- **Single-word labeling cards** — printable, big image, big word
- **Songs with multi-step gestures** — Old MacDonald, Five Little Ducks
- **Knob puzzles + simple jigsaws** — 4-6 pieces with whole shapes
- **Sorting bins** — by color, by size, by category (animals vs. food)
- **First "stories"** — extremely short, repetitive, with a sound to make on each page
- **Sensory bin invitations** — themed: "ocean" with blue rice + sea creature toys
- **Indoor scavenger hunt** — "find one round thing, one soft thing, one yellow thing"
- **Movement prompts** — animal walks (bear, crab, bunny), freeze dance

## Skip these

- Mazes (motor planning isn't there)
- Mad libs (no)
- Multi-step crafts requiring waiting
- Detailed coloring pages
- Long stories with plot
- Anything assuming letter recognition (rare at this age — don't pressure)
- Win/lose games — they don't get the rules and feelings will run hot

## Print packet style

- One big main invitation; one small "extra" (a song, a finger play, a single picture to point to)
- Visuals dominate; words are minimal and read by the parent
- Add a "ways to extend" section so engaged kids can keep going for 20 minutes
- Grown-up footer: what to look for, what's developmentally exciting

## Frameworks fit

- **Montessori**: gold standard. Practical life. "Help me" invitations. Real tools.
- **Hunt Gather Parent**: teach by working alongside. "Let me show you how to fold this."
- **Good Inside**: emotion-naming scripts ("I see you're frustrated. The puzzle is hard. I'm right here.")
- **RIE**: continues — uninterrupted play, narration of care
- **Waldorf**: nature walks, gentle rhythm, simple wooden play

## Learning goal nudges

- 100+ word vocabulary, then 200+
- Two- to three-word phrases
- Following 2-step instructions ("get the cup and bring it to me")
- Pretend play sequences
- Identifying 3-4 colors
- Counting to 5 (rote)
- Pointing to named body parts (5+)
- First scribble-letters that look like attempts

## Sensitivities watch

- "No" phase can be intense — *the activity must allow them to refuse and come back*. Don't make a 22-month-old commit
- Sharing is mostly impossible — solo packets only
- Big transitions (end of activity, going to a new room) need warning
- Naptime crashes a packet — keep it short enough to fit before naps
