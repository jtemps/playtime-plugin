# Age 0-6 months

This is mostly **caregiver-with-baby** time. The "packet" is a guide for the grown-up — what to set up, do, or say *with* the baby. The baby is the audience, not the operator.

## What's happening developmentally

- Brain is doubling in size; visual system maturing fast (high-contrast in first 8 weeks, color around 3-4mo, depth perception around 5mo)
- Tracking faces, then objects; fixates ~12 inches in early weeks
- Cooing → babbling (around 4mo); reciprocal sounds with caregivers
- Tummy time → head control → rolling (one direction by ~4mo, both by ~6mo)
- Object reach (3-4mo), grasping (4-5mo), transferring object hand-to-hand (~6mo)
- Social smile (~6-8 weeks); belly laugh (~4mo)
- Object permanence is NOT yet established — out of sight is gone

## What works

- **High-contrast cards** (black/white/red), held 8-12" away, for sub-3-month-olds. We can generate printable contrast cards.
- **Tummy time prompts** with reasons to lift the head — a contrast image to look at, a slowly-shaken rattle prompt for the parent
- **Face-to-face "conversation"** scripts — back-and-forth babble suggestions
- **Songs and rhymes** — printed lyrics + suggested gestures (Itsy Bitsy Spider, Pat-a-Cake, This Little Piggy)
- **Sensory invitations** — textures to touch (silk, terry cloth, wood), simple
- **Mirror play** — print a "baby mirror moments" prompt sheet
- **Movement games** — gentle bicycle legs, tracking a slow-moving object

## Skip these

- Anything requiring sitting (until ~6mo)
- Anything with small parts (choking)
- Mad libs, puzzles, mazes — none of this exists yet
- Long activities — attention spans are minutes, not 20 minutes

## Print packet style for 0-6mo

The packet is **for the parent**, formatted as a one-page "morning invitation":

- Suggested 3-4 micro-moments to do across the morning (each 2-5 minutes)
- A printable contrast card or tummy-time prompt
- A song/rhyme with gestures
- A "what to notice" tip from baby's developmental window (e.g. "at 4mo, watch them try to swat at toys — that's a HUGE coordination win")

## Frameworks fit

- **RIE / Magda Gerber**: massive fit at this age. Slow, narrate care, uninterrupted floor time.
- **Montessori**: Munari/Gobbi mobiles, treasure baskets (older end of this range).
- **Waldorf**: silk play, nature objects, gentle song.
- **Hunt Gather Parent**: emphasis on calm, low-stimulation, baby-wearing.

## Learning goal nudges

- Tummy time minutes per day
- Tracking objects across midline
- Reciprocal cooing (turn-taking)
- Reaching and grasping
- Belly laughing (it's a real win)
