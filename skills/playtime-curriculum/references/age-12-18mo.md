# Age 12-18 months

Walking + first words + WILL. The toddler's Big Bang. Imitation is the dominant learning mode. Schemas (transporting, enclosing, trajectory, rotation) start showing up — *follow the schema, ride the obsession*.

## What's happening developmentally

- **12-14mo**: walking (most), 1-3 true words, points to body parts on request, puts objects in containers
- **14-16mo**: combining two words ("more milk"), scribbling with whole-arm motion, uses spoon (messily), 5-15 word vocabulary
- **16-18mo**: 20-50+ words, brief pretend play (feeding a doll, pretending to talk on a phone), stacks 3-4 blocks, identifies pictures in books
- Schemas active: transporting (carrying things place to place), enclosing (putting self in boxes), trajectory (throwing), rotation (spinning)
- Joint attention is rich — they look at what you're looking at
- Big feelings + tiny coping skills — meltdowns are normal
- **Receptive >> expressive** still — they understand WAY more than they say

## What works

- **One-step instructions with imitation** — "can you give the bear a bite?"
- **Scribbling** — chunky crayons or jumbo markers; big paper. We can prompt the *invitation*, not the output
- **Sensory bins** — supervised, dyed rice or beans (large enough not to choke), measuring cups, a scoop
- **Sticker peeling** — pre-pulled stickers on a sheet, a target shape to fill (huge fine motor win)
- **Container play** — sorting big chunky items into 2 bins by color (with parent modeling)
- **Pretend play seeds** — "today's invitation: a doll, a small bowl, a spoon — feeding time"
- **Posting / dropping** — coin slots, ball drops, mail slot from a cardboard box
- **Knob puzzles** — 1-3 piece, single object per piece
- **Schemas catalog** — propose activities matching the kid's current schema obsession
- **Walk-around scavenger hunt** — find 3 things that are red, in a small space
- **Songs with motions** — Head Shoulders Knees Toes (gentle), Wheels on the Bus
- **Picture book "name it" pages** — printable big-image-single-word cards
- **Pour-and-scoop play** — water table, dry beans (supervised)

## Skip these

- Anything with output expectations ("draw a circle" — no)
- Mazes, mad libs, multi-step puzzles
- Scissors (mostly), glue (mostly)
- Stories with plot — they want naming and pointing, not narrative
- Long sit-down activities (>10 min)
- Detailed coloring pages (way too small)
- Choking-hazard small parts

## Print packet style

- A single invitation per page, with parent-prep instructions (60-second setup)
- Big simple visual on the page (a single object, an animal, a face) — at toddler eye level
- 1-2 lines of "what to say while doing it"
- A schema callout if relevant ("this hits transporting + posting — perfect for [Kid]'s current obsession")
- A grown-up footer: what they're learning + what to celebrate

## Frameworks fit

- **Montessori**: PEAK fit. Practical life invitations (pouring, sweeping, plant-watering), heuristic play
- **RIE**: respectful narration, "I'm going to pick you up now"
- **Hunt Gather Parent**: TEAM — autonomy + minimal interference, "let them try"
- **Good Inside**: scripts for the meltdown that WILL come — "you wanted X; you cannot have X; I am here"
- **Simplicity Parenting**: less stuff out, deeper play

## Learning goal nudges

- Walking confidently
- 20+ word vocabulary
- Following 1-step instructions
- Scribbling
- First pretend play moments
- Drinking from open cup (if family does that)
- Pointing to body parts

## Sensitivities watch

- BIG transitions are hard — packets that require switching activities mid-stream often flop
- Crowds and overstimulation — keep packets quiet for sensitive kids
- Hunger / nap timing matters more than activity quality at this age
