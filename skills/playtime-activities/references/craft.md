# Craft

## What it is

A make-something-with-your-hands project. Real materials. Real finished thing. Tactile, satisfying, takes 10-45 minutes depending on age.

## Age range

**2-12 years**.

## Skills it builds

- Fine motor
- Sequencing / following instructions
- Spatial reasoning
- Patience
- Pride of making (huge for confidence)

## Format spec

The packet is a **printable instruction sheet** (and possibly templates to cut out). Required:

- Title — fun, kid-named ("Maya's Cardboard Submarine")
- **Materials list**, exact quantities — must match what's in `household.materials`
- **Optional materials** — for extending
- **Step-by-step instructions** with picture-icons for under-6, text + icons for over-6
- **Number of steps**: 2-3 (toddler), 4-6 (preschool), 6-10 (school-age), 10+ (older with breaks)
- **Time estimate** — honest
- **Difficulty** — easy/medium/hard, with reasoning
- **Adult help required?** — explicit for sharp things, hot things, glue gun, sewing
- **Templates** to cut out (when relevant) — print-ready on the packet itself
- **A "what to do with it" suggestion** — display, gift, play with, photograph

## Quality bar

- **The finished thing is genuinely cool** — kid will want to show it off
- **Material list is REAL** — only stuff the family said they have
- **Steps are clear and sequenced** — no "now somehow attach the wings"
- **Templates are accurate** — proper proportions, ready to cut
- **Failure-resistant** — if step 4 goes wrong, the project still works
- **Personalized** — "your dragon" not "a dragon"; theme-aligned; interest-aligned
- **Mess-aware** — flag what'll get messy and how to set up
- **Finishable** — the kid finishes it in one sitting (or it's explicitly multi-day)

## Variations

- **Recyclable craft** — toilet paper rolls, egg cartons, cereal boxes
- **Paper craft** — fold, cut, glue paper into 3D shapes
- **Sewing craft** (older) — felt, needle, thread
- **Painting craft** — process art, watercolor, finger paint (toddler)
- **Building craft** — LEGO, cardboard, blocks
- **Sensory craft** — playdough sculptures, slime, oobleck
- **Wearable craft** — paper crown, mask, friendship bracelet
- **Puppet craft** — paper bag, sock, finger puppet
- **Holiday/seasonal craft**

## Material requirements (catalog)

Common toolkit assumed:
- Printer + scissors + tape + glue stick + crayons/markers
- Paper: copy paper, construction paper, cardstock

Plus household-stocked extras (check `household.materials`):
- Pipe cleaners, googly eyes, pom-poms, stickers
- Yarn, string
- Cotton balls
- Recyclables (TP rolls, egg cartons, boxes, jars)
- Paint (acrylic, watercolor, finger)
- Foam, felt
- Beads, sequins
- Natural materials (leaves, sticks, rocks, pinecones)

## Print considerations

- Templates in clean black outline, scaled accurately
- Step instructions: numbered, bold step number, picture-icon if young
- Color mode: color illustrations if available, but works in B&W
- Multi-page acceptable — tell the user the page count

## Example brief

> Kid: Maya, 4yr
> Theme: ocean
> Materials: printer, crayons, scissors, tape, paper, recyclables (cardboard tube saved)

→ Title: "Maya's Megalodon Cardboard Submarine"
→ Materials: 1 cardboard tube, 1 sheet construction paper (any color), tape, scissors (with help), crayons, the printable templates below
→ Time: 25 min
→ Difficulty: medium (cutting curves)
→ Steps:
1. Cut out the periscope, fin, and porthole templates [icons]
2. Color them in (porthole = window — what's inside?)
3. Wrap construction paper around the tube and tape
4. Tape the porthole, fin, and periscope to the tube
5. Roar like a megalodon and dive!
→ Templates included: 1 fin (curve), 1 periscope (rectangle + circle on top), 2 portholes (circles)
→ Footer: "Tomorrow: bath-time test — does it float?"
