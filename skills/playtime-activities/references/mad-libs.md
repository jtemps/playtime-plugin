# Mad libs

## What it is

A short story with strategic words removed. The kid (or a grown-up reading the prompts) supplies words by part of speech without seeing the story; the result is a hilarious random text. Best read aloud at the end.

## Age range

**5-12 years**. Earlier (3-4) requires picture-aided word banks; older (10-12) can be sophisticated and ironic.

## Skills it builds

- Parts of speech recognition
- Vocabulary
- Reading comprehension (when reading the result)
- Wordplay + humor
- Listening for context

## Format spec

A **short, snappy story** (60-200 words depending on age) with **6-15 blanks** strategically placed for maximum humor. Each blank is labeled with its part-of-speech requirement.

```
Once upon a time, a __________ (adjective) __________ (animal) lived in a tiny __________ (place). Every morning it would __________ (verb, -ing) until it noticed something __________ (adjective)...
```

### Layout

Two-section page:

1. **Top half — the word list** (kid fills in WITHOUT seeing the story). Numbered:
   1. An adjective: ___________
   2. An animal: ___________
   3. A place: ___________
   4. A verb ending in -ing: ___________
   5. ...
2. **Bottom half — the story** with blanks numbered. Big enough font to read aloud easily.

For younger kids (5-7), include a **picture word-bank** alongside each blank with 4-6 options to circle. (E.g. for "an animal", show pictures of common animals.)

## Quality bar

- The **story is good even before filled in** — funny premise, kid-relatable, theme-aligned
- **Strategic blank placement** — the funny words are nouns, adjectives, body parts, sounds, exclamations. Connectors and articles stay in
- **A mix of parts of speech** so the result is varied
- **A built-in punchline** — the last blank lands a laugh
- **No off-color humor** without parental green-light; bathroom humor is fine for ages 5-8 in moderation
- **Personalization**: kid's name, family member names, pet names can be in the story (NOT in blanks)

## Variations

- **Choose-your-own mad lib**: 2-3 mini stories, kid picks which to fill in
- **Family mad lib**: each family member fills in 2 words
- **Theme-anchored**: ocean mad lib, dinosaur mad lib, knight mad lib
- **Letter to a stuffed animal mad lib**

## Material requirements

Printer + pen/pencil. That's it.

## Print considerations

- Generous spacing on the blank lines (2× the line height)
- Bold or boxed parts-of-speech labels
- Story font: serif, readable, 14-16pt
- Color mode: minimal — mostly black on white; small color accents OK

## Example brief

> Kid: Theo, 7yr
> Interests: dragons, dinosaurs
> Theme: medieval castle
> Frameworks: Good Inside

→ Title: "The Dragon Who Couldn't ___ (verb)"
→ A 150-word story about a dragon learning a feeling. 9 blanks, including: name, adjective × 2, body part, food × 2, sound, color, verb ending in -ing.
→ Punchline blank: a SOUND in the very last sentence ("...and that's how the dragon learned that even big feelings can ____ (sound) into something new.")
→ Good-Inside hat tip: the story models naming a feeling, even via comedy.
