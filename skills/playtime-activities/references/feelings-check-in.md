# Feelings check-in

## What it is

A printable page that invites the kid to identify and name how they're feeling — through faces, colors, weather metaphors, body maps, or stories. Builds emotional vocabulary and self-awareness.

## Age range

**2-12 years**, scaled significantly.

## Skills it builds

- Emotional vocabulary
- Self-awareness / interoception
- Co-regulation (when done with a parent)
- Empathy

## Format spec

| Age | Format |
|---|---|
| 2-3 yr | 4 big face circles (happy, sad, mad, scared) — "point to how you feel" |
| 3-5 yr | 6-8 face circles + a simple body map ("color where you feel it") |
| 5-8 yr | Emotion wheel (12 feelings) + "what's making you feel that way?" prompt |
| 8-12 yr | Full SIFT or similar — sensations / images / feelings / thoughts |

Each version includes:

- A title — gentle, inviting ("How's Theo Feeling Today?")
- The check-in itself
- A "what could help right now?" prompt (write, draw, ask, take a walk, hug)
- A grown-up footer with framework-aligned scripts

## Quality bar

- **Faces are warm and real** — not generic emoticon
- **No "right" answer** — every feeling is welcomed
- **No judgment language** — no "good" or "bad" feelings
- **Body map is tasteful** — abstract outline; no detail that could feel weird
- **Frameworks woven in**:
  - Good Inside: "two things are true" prompt
  - Whole-Brain Child: name-it-to-tame-it
  - Faber-Mazlish: "you're feeling ___" mirror script

## Variations

- **Weather metaphor** — "today I feel like: sunshine / cloudy / rainstorm / lightning / fog"
- **Color metaphor** — "if today were a color, what color is it? color the page"
- **Animal metaphor** — "what animal feels like me today?"
- **Inside/outside** — "outside I look like ___; inside I feel like ___"
- **Big-feeling story** — a short story prompt about a character with a feeling, then "have you ever felt like that?"
- **Worry box** — "what's a worry I want to put down today?"
- **Gratitude flip** — "what's something good?"

## Material requirements

Printer + pencil/crayons.

## Print considerations

- Soft, warm illustration style
- Generous whitespace
- Color mode: feelings-wheel benefits from color, but B&W with hatching works
- One activity per page

## Example brief (for a 4-yr-old after a big-feelings week)

→ Title: "How's Theo Today?"
→ 6 face circles (happy, sad, mad, worried, excited, tired) — kid circles or colors
→ A body outline: "color where you feel it. arrows where it goes."
→ A "what could help" line with 4 picture-icons: hug, draw, breathe (with parent), tell someone
→ Footer (Good-Inside aligned): "💛 Two things are true: you can have any feeling AND you are deeply loved."
