# Science experiment

## What it is

A safe, kitchen-doable experiment with a real "why does that happen?" payoff. Includes hypothesis prompts for older kids; pure delight-and-noticing for younger ones.

## Age range

**3-12 years**.

## Skills it builds

- Observation
- Prediction / hypothesis
- Cause and effect
- Patience (for slow experiments)
- Vocabulary (real science terms used age-appropriately)
- Persistence (when an experiment goes sideways)

## Format spec

A **single-page experiment sheet** with:

- Title — fun + scientific ("The Great Magic Milk Mystery")
- **Question** at the top: "What happens if we mix dish soap with milk and food coloring?"
- **Materials list** — exact quantities, kitchen-stocked
- **Time** — including any waiting (some take overnight)
- **Adult help needed?** — flag fire, knives, electrical
- **Steps** — numbered, illustrated, picture-icons for under-6
- **Predict box** (older) — "what do you think will happen?" with space to write/draw
- **Observe box** — "what did you see?" with space to write/draw
- **The why** — a kid-language explanation of the science (1-3 sentences)
- **Try this next** — variation suggestions

## Quality bar

- **Safe.** Always. No fire without explicit adult-supervision flag; no toxic combinations
- **Reliable.** It actually works. Test the recipe in your head — if water needs to be hot, say so
- **Magic-feeling.** The result is visually surprising or delightful
- **Real science.** A real phenomenon (surface tension, density, chemistry, biology, optics) — not a "wow" with no explanation
- **Explanation is honest** — kid-level but accurate. Don't say "magic" if it's chemistry
- **Material-check** — only what's in the household
- **Mess-aware** — flag setup ("do this on a baking tray" / "outside")

## Catalog of reliable experiments by age

### 3-5 yr (sensory + cause-effect)

- **Magic milk** — milk + food coloring + dish soap = swirling colors
- **Sink or float** — household objects in a bowl of water
- **Color mixing** — water + food coloring in cups, mix to discover
- **Dancing raisins** — raisins + clear soda = bobbing raisins
- **Salt vs. sugar** — taste; observe melting on warm surface
- **Static balloon** — rub balloon, pick up paper bits
- **Frozen-and-thaw** — observe an ice cube melt
- **Ramps and rolling** — what rolls fastest down a slope?
- **Rainbow walking water** — paper towels + cups of colored water
- **Volcano** — baking soda + vinegar (classic, still amazing)

### 5-8 yr (observation + prediction)

- **Density column** — honey, syrup, oil, water, alcohol layered in a glass
- **Bouncy egg** — soak egg in vinegar overnight; shell dissolves
- **Invisible ink** — lemon juice + heat = brown writing
- **Crystal growing** — salt or sugar + warm water; crystallize over days
- **Plant transpiration** — celery in colored water; watch the leaves change color
- **Chromatography** — coffee filter + marker + water; colors separate
- **Tornado in a bottle** — two bottles + water + connector
- **Egg in a bottle** — peeled boiled egg + match-warmed bottle
- **Solar prints** — sun-print paper + objects (or construction paper + objects in sun)
- **Penny shine** — vinegar + salt; clean dirty pennies
- **Erupting snowman** — warmer-weather kid-friendly chemistry
- **DIY sundial** — using a stick and chalk

### 8-12 yr (real science notebook)

- **DIY pH indicator** — red cabbage + various test liquids
- **Polymer slime** — borax (with care) or contact-lens solution
- **Yeast balloon** — yeast + sugar + water inflates a balloon
- **Mentos + diet cola** (outside)
- **Egg drop challenge** — engineering meets gravity
- **Crystal radio** (with adult)
- **Simple circuits** — playdough + LED + battery
- **Surface tension** — pepper + water + dish soap; coins on a water drop
- **Optical illusions** — afterimage, color spinning disks
- **Solar oven** — pizza box + foil

## Variations

- **Series across a week** — a "lab notebook" with one experiment per day
- **Predict-then-test** — kid writes prediction; tests; revises
- **Pair-with-a-book** — link to a children's science book
- **Outdoor science** — nature observation as experiment
- **Backyard biology** — bug hunt with sketching

## Material requirements

Per experiment. Always within `household.materials`.

## Print considerations

- Step icons + numbers
- Color photos/diagrams of the expected result for under-8
- Lab-notebook style for older — predict / observe / explain blocks
- Color mode: color illustrations of expected results help; B&W version with descriptive labels works fine

## Example brief

> Kid: Theo, 7yr
> Interests: dragons, dinosaurs, fire (typical)
> Theme: chemistry week
> Materials: kitchen stocked

→ Title: "Theo's Dragon-Breath Experiment" (= baking soda volcano with twist)
→ Question: "Can we make 'dragon breath' in the kitchen?"
→ Materials: 2 tbsp baking soda, 1/4 cup vinegar, dish soap, red food coloring, a tall narrow cup, a tray to catch the mess
→ Steps + Predict box + Observe box + The Why (acid + base = CO2 gas) + Try Next (warmer vinegar = bigger reaction; add Mentos to soda for comparison)
