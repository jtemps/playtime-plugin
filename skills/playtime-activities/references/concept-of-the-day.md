# Concept of the day

## What it is

A single concept introduced through a story, an example, a picture, and a short activity. The concept can be a virtue (perseverance, kindness), an emotion (envy, pride), a vocabulary word, a science idea (gravity, magnetism), or a cultural concept (interdependence, generosity).

## Age range

**2-12 years**, scaled.

## Skills it builds

- Vocabulary
- Conceptual understanding
- Reflection
- Connection-making (linking concepts to own life)

## Format spec

A **single-page introduction** with:

- The **concept word** big at the top
- A **kid-language definition** — 1-2 sentences
- A **picture or visual metaphor** — illustrated
- A **mini-story** (50-150 words) showing the concept in action
- A **prompt** — "today, look for [concept] in real life", "draw a time you [concept-d]", "ask a grown-up about a time they [concept-d]"
- For 5+: a **noticing journal** — write/draw an example you find today
- For 8+: a **deeper reflection** — what does this concept mean to you?

## Quality bar

- **Honest** — not preachy, not saccharine
- **Concrete examples** kids can actually picture
- **Not adult abstraction** — "perseverance" for a 4yr = "keeping trying when something is hard"
- **Story shows, doesn't tell** — character does the thing; we don't lecture
- **Inviting prompt** — they want to look for it

## Vocabulary words (alternative to virtues)

Pick fun, concrete, slightly elevated words for the kid's age:

- 4-6 yr: enormous, microscopic, ancient, drowsy, gleaming, vibrant, melancholy (yes, give them a feeling word!)
- 6-9 yr: ferocious, magnificent, tranquil, peculiar, brilliant, ridiculous, grateful, enthusiastic
- 9-12 yr: serendipity, ephemeral, perspective, paradox, quintessential, juxtaposition, ineffable

## Variations

- **Word of the day** — vocabulary focus, etymology, "use it 3 times today"
- **Virtue of the week** — same concept across 5-7 days, deepening
- **Concept + experiment** (science concept paired with hands-on)
- **Concept + drawing** ("draw what kindness looks like")
- **Concept + interview** ("ask a grown-up what perseverance means to them")
- **3-period vocabulary** (Montessori) — "this is patience" → "show me patience" → "what is this?"

## Material requirements

Printer + pencil. Optionally journal/notebook.

## Print considerations

- Concept word in big, friendly typography at top
- Story in serif body
- Reflection prompts in handwritten-feel italic or boxed
- Color mode: 1-2 accent colors at most; mostly text-driven

## Example brief

> Kid: Maya, 4yr
> Today's concept: BRAVERY
> Frameworks: Good Inside

→ Top: "BRAVERY"
→ Definition: "Bravery isn't being unafraid. Bravery is doing something that feels scary when you decide it matters."
→ Picture: a small kid in too-big rain boots stepping into a puddle that looks like an ocean
→ Mini-story: "Maya stood at the edge of the slide. It looked TALL. Her belly felt fluttery. 'You don't have to,' said Mom. Maya thought about it. Then she said, 'I'm scared AND I want to.' She climbed up. She slid. She laughed."
→ Prompt: "Today, look for someone being brave. It might be small."
→ Footer (Good-Inside): "💛 'Scared AND ___' is the whole thing. Two things can be true."
