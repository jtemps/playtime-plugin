# Pretend play scenario

## What it is

A printable "play kit" — characters, setting, problem, props — that scaffolds a pretend-play scene. The kid runs the scene; the page is a launching pad.

## Age range

**2-8 years.**

## Skills it builds

- Imagination
- Language (rich pretend talk)
- Theory of mind (taking on roles)
- Sequencing (scene structure)
- Social-emotional rehearsal (working out feelings via play)

## Format spec

A **single-page scene-setter** with:

- Scenario title — fun and specific ("The Vet Clinic on Bug Street")
- **Setting** — described in 1-2 sentences + a small illustration
- **Characters** — 2-4 with names and a hint of personality (kid plays one or rotates)
- **Problem / mission** — what's happening; what needs to be solved
- **Suggested props** — household items only (a blanket, a basket, a wooden spoon)
- **Story sparks** — 3-5 things that *might* happen ("a customer arrives with a sneezing dragon", "the medicine cabinet is empty")
- **Optional: cut-out character cards** for finger puppets / paper figures

## Quality bar

- **The kid drives the play** — we set the stage; they take it from there
- **Open-ended problem** — not a fixed "solve this"; many endings are possible
- **Material-light** — nothing the family doesn't have
- **Theme-aligned + interest-aligned**
- **Frameworks woven in naturally**:
  - Hunt-Gather: alongside-grown-ups roles ("today you're the family chef and Mom is the assistant")
  - Reggio: "what's your theory about how the dragon got sick?"
  - Good Inside: a character has a feeling that's named and tended

## Scenario catalog

### 2-3 yr

- Feeding the bear / doll
- Doctor checks the teddy
- Going to the grocery store with a basket
- Cooking soup in a pot
- Putting baby to bed

### 3-5 yr

- Vet clinic
- Restaurant (kid is chef AND server)
- Post office (deliver letters around the house)
- Garage / mechanic shop
- Spaceship (couch is the cockpit)
- Pirate ship (cardboard box = boat)
- Library (kid is the librarian)
- Hairdresser
- Construction site
- Castle with a king/queen/dragon
- Forest adventure
- Underwater exploration
- Birthday party for a stuffed animal

### 5-8 yr

- Detective agency (real mystery to solve)
- Newspaper office (write a story)
- TV cooking show
- Magic potion shop
- Time traveler (visit a historical era)
- Olympics
- Award ceremony
- Animal rescue HQ
- Science lab
- Travel agency (plan a fictional trip)
- News broadcast
- Theater rehearsal

## Material requirements

Printer + paper + scissors (for cut-outs). Household props named in scenario.

## Print considerations

- One activity per page; cut-outs on a second optional page
- Characters drawn warmly; not cartoonish; not "themed" with commercial characters
- Color mode: color illustrations preferable but B&W works
- Generous whitespace

## Example brief

> Kid: Maya, 4yr
> Interests: ocean, vets, helping
> Theme: ocean week

→ Title: "Maya's Underwater Vet Clinic"
→ Setting: "Deep below the waves, a tiny clinic helps sick sea creatures. Today, business is slow... until a knock on the door."
→ Characters:
  - Dr. Maya — the vet (you!)
  - Stella the sea star — quiet, has lost an arm
  - Captain Crab — chatty, has a pinchy claw
  - Bubbles the baby octopus — wiggly, has the hiccups
→ Problem: Three patients arrive at once. Who do you help first?
→ Suggested props: a basket (clinic), a blanket (the ocean floor), 3 stuffies (patients), a flashlight (the underwater lamp)
→ Sparks:
  - Stella shares a secret
  - Captain Crab won't sit still
  - Bubbles' hiccups get LOUDER and bigger
  - A surprise patient arrives with a song
→ Cut-outs: 3 character cards (printable on page 2 if family likes paper figures)
