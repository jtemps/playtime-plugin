# Scavenger hunt

## What it is

A list of things to find — pictures, items, sounds, sensations — around the house, yard, or on a walk. Kid checks them off as they go.

## Age range

**2-12 years**.

## Skills it builds

- Observation
- Vocabulary
- Movement (active hunting)
- Categorization
- Curiosity

## Format spec

A **single-page hunt list** with:

- Title — themed ("Theo's Backyard Bug Patrol")
- **Setting** — where to hunt (house, yard, park, neighborhood)
- **Item list** — 6-15 items depending on age, each with:
  - A picture (under-6: required; older: nice-to-have)
  - A short label
  - A checkbox
- **Bonus / "if you can find it"** items — 1-3 hard ones for extension
- **Time / pace guidance** — "no rush; come back over the morning"
- **A wrap-up prompt** — draw your favorite, tell a grown-up about one, photograph it

## Quality bar

- **Findable** — all items are realistically available in the setting
- **Theme-aligned** — items connect to the day's theme or kid's interest
- **Mixed difficulty** — some easy ("a leaf"), some interesting ("a leaf with a hole in it")
- **Not just "find a [thing]"** — better: "find something soft", "find something that makes a sound when you tap it"
- **Picture aids** for younger kids; older kids can do text-only with style
- **Outdoor-aware** — if the family doesn't have outdoor access, default to indoor

## Variations

- **Sense-based** — find something rough, smooth, fragrant, loud, cold, sweet
- **Color hunt** — find one of every color of the rainbow
- **Number hunt** — find one of, two of, three of...
- **Letter hunt** — find something starting with each letter of the kid's name
- **Texture hunt** — different feels under fingertips
- **Sound hunt** — different sounds to listen for
- **Photo hunt** (older) — kid takes photos with parent's phone
- **Nature hunt** — leaf, rock, bug, bird, flower, cloud shape, animal track
- **Indoor hunt** — household items by category, "things in our kitchen that are round"
- **Walking-route hunt** — done on a walk; visual + observational
- **Family hunt** — find a thing each family member loves

## Material requirements

Printer + pencil. Optionally: small bag/basket to collect findings; phone for photos (older).

## Print considerations

- Big checkboxes
- Picture-icons for under-7
- One activity per page
- Color: optional; works well in B&W with line art

## Example brief

> Kid: Theo, 5yr
> Interests: bugs, dinosaurs
> Setting: backyard + house
> Theme: nature week

→ Title: "Theo's Bug Patrol Mission"
→ Setting: backyard (with house as backup if too cold)
→ List of 10:
  ☐ A bug with wings (picture: butterfly silhouette)
  ☐ A bug with NO wings (picture: ant)
  ☐ Something a bug could hide under (picture: rock)
  ☐ A leaf with a hole in it (picture: leaf with bite)
  ☐ A spiderweb (picture)
  ☐ A line of ants (picture)
  ☐ Something yellow (picture: dandelion)
  ☐ A bug's footprint or trail (picture: trail in dirt)
  ☐ A tiny rock that fits in your palm (picture)
  ☐ Something a beetle might eat (picture: leaf with hole)
→ Bonus: ☐ A slug! (picture)
→ Wrap-up: "Pick your favorite finding and draw it on the back of this page."
