# Comic strip

## What it is

A printable comic-strip template — empty panels with speech bubbles — that the kid fills in to tell their own story.

## Age range

**4-12 years.**

## Skills it builds

- Sequencing / narrative structure
- Drawing
- Writing (speech bubbles)
- Pacing (one moment per panel)
- Storytelling

## Format spec

A **single-page template** with:

- Title bar at top — "[Kid's name]'s Comic" with space to write a title
- **Panel grid** sized for the age:
  | Age | Panels | Layout |
  |---|---|---|
  | 4-5 yr | 3 | One row of 3 large squares |
  | 5-6 yr | 4 | 2x2 grid |
  | 6-8 yr | 6 | 3x2 grid |
  | 8-12 yr | 6-9 | 3x2 or 3x3, varied panel sizes |
- **Empty speech bubbles** in some panels (older kids draw their own)
- **Story prompt** at the bottom — one to choose from, or open-ended
- For young kids: a tiny how-to for drawing simple characters

## Quality bar

- **Empty enough to be theirs** — we don't draw the story
- **Structured enough to scaffold** — bubbles, panel borders, a starting prompt
- **Real prompt** — not "draw anything"; give a hook ("the day you discovered the hidden door...")
- **Fun side prompts** — sound effects bank ("BOOM, ZAP, KAPOW, SQUISH"), expression cheat sheet (faces with feelings)

## Variations

- **Half-finished comic** — first panel drawn for them; they finish
- **Story sequence** — 3 random images at top; kid arranges them in panels and writes
- **Sound-effect comic** — focus is the SFX; kid finds the loudest moment
- **Photo comic** — kid takes 4 photos then prints/draws over them
- **Family member as protagonist** — Grandma's secret superpower
- **Themed comic** — "an animal you've never met"

## Material requirements

Printer + pencil + crayons/markers.

## Print considerations

- Panel borders thick (2-3px)
- Speech bubble outlines pre-drawn but empty
- Plenty of whitespace inside panels (they need room to draw)
- Color mode: B&W works perfectly; color is for kid

## Example brief

> Kid: Theo, 7yr
> Interests: dragons, knights, mystery
> Theme: bravery

→ Title bar: "Theo's Comic: ___________"
→ 6 panels, 3x2 grid
→ Story prompt: "Sir Theo finds a door inside the castle wall that nobody has noticed before. What's behind it?"
→ Speech bubbles in panels 2, 4, 6
→ Bottom-right corner: SFX bank (POOF, CRASH, GASP, ZOOM, TAP TAP)
→ Bottom-left: "Faces" cheat sheet — surprised, scared, brave, curious, joyful
