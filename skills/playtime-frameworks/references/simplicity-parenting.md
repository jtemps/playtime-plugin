# Simplicity Parenting (Kim John Payne)

**Core idea**: Modern childhood is over-stimulated, over-scheduled, and over-toy'd. The cure is **less**: fewer toys, slower rhythm, more space, more boredom, more nature. Childhood is a slow unfolding, not an enrichment race.

## Pillars

- **Simplify the environment**: fewer toys, fewer choices, less clutter — kids play deeper with less
- **Establish rhythms**: predictable daily / weekly rhythms anchor kids; novelty becomes precious
- **Schedule less**: protect free time, boredom, family meals
- **Filter media**: thoughtful, slow, sparing
- **Slow down the pace**: less rushing, fewer transitions, more presence

## Design implications for packets

- **One main activity, period.** Don't over-pack the page
- **Lots of whitespace**: visually calm
- **Repeating rituals**: a "Tuesday is hot-cocoa-and-story morning" rhythm element woven across weeks
- **Boredom-tolerance prompts**: "lie on the floor and look at the ceiling for 5 minutes — then start"
- **Fewer materials**: pick activities that need 2-3 things, not 10
- **Nature emphasis**: outdoor and observation activities highlighted
- **Slow framing**: "you don't need to finish today" "do this for as long as feels right"

## Sample parent-footer line

> 🌾 **A Simplicity note**: it's okay if [Kid] only does part of this. Slow is the point.

## Activity types that fit especially well

Nature observation · weather journal · gratitude journal · simple drawing prompts · long-form crafts (across days) · single-material art · cooking together · family rhythms (a "Sunday soup" prompt) · outdoor play prompts

## What to avoid

- Multi-component crafts with 10 materials
- Over-packed pages
- Speed framing
- Heavy media references
- "Kids will love this!" hype
