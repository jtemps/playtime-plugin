# Montessori (Maria Montessori)

**Core idea**: Children have an innate drive to learn; the adult's job is to prepare a beautiful, ordered environment with real tools, then *follow the child*. Practical life is the foundation; cosmic education is the horizon.

## Pillars

- **Prepared environment**: child-sized furniture, low shelves, accessible materials, beauty matters
- **Follow the child**: observe interests; offer materials matched to sensitive periods
- **Practical life**: real tasks (pouring, cutting, sweeping, dressing) come first
- **Sensorial work**: refining the senses through specific materials (color, sound, texture, weight, dimension)
- **Concrete before abstract**: math, language, geography all start with materials you can touch
- **Three-period lesson**: 1) "This is X" 2) "Show me X" 3) "What is this?" — used for new vocabulary
- **Control of error**: materials are self-correcting (a peg won't fit if you've stacked them wrong)
- **Mixed ages**: older kids teach younger
- **Concentration is sacred**: don't interrupt a child working
- **Cosmic education** (6+): the big picture — universe, life, humans, humans' work

## Design implications for packets

- **Real-tool framing**: scissors are scissors, not "kid scissors"; we use a real tomato, not a plastic one
- **Practical-life invitations**: setting the table, peeling, sorting, polishing
- **Sensorial work**: 0-3 yr basket of textures; 3-6 yr color-grading exercises
- **Self-correcting design**: connect-the-dots with a clear answer; matching pages where wrong matches are obvious
- **Three-period vocabulary lessons** in concept-of-the-day
- **No interruption**: parent footer instructs the grown-up to step back during concentration
- **Beauty**: clean, uncluttered design; whitespace; quality typography
- **Older-kid cosmic content**: Great Lessons, timeline of life, geography, "what is the sun?"

## Sample parent-footer line

> ✨ **A Montessori note**: when [Kid] is concentrating, please don't interrupt. The work of focus is more valuable than the finished result.

## Activity types that fit especially well

Practical life · sensorial bins · letter and number tracing (with sandpaper-letter feel) · 3-period concept-of-the-day · matching · sorting · cooking · gardening · geography · how-to · zoology research · timeline-of-life prompts

## What to avoid

- Fantasy-heavy themes for under-6 (Montessori traditionally favors reality first; soften this if the family wants — many modern Montessorians embrace fantasy too)
- Plastic / themed-character materials in instructions
- Adult-heavy direction
- Praise inflation
- Speed framing ("how fast can you?")
