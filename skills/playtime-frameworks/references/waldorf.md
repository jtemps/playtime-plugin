# Waldorf / Steiner

**Core idea**: Childhood is a sacred, slow unfolding. Imagination, beauty, rhythm, and natural materials nourish the child's developing inner life. Academics wait until they're developmentally ready (around 7); imagination and rhythm come first.

## Pillars

- **Rhythm**: predictable daily / weekly / seasonal rhythm anchors the child
- **Imitation, then imagination**: 0-7 yr learns by imitating; imagination flowers in this same period
- **Natural materials**: wool, silk, wood, beeswax — open-ended, beautiful
- **Open-ended toys**: a silk is a cape, a sea, a baby blanket — toys with form-given-by-the-child
- **Storytelling > screens**: oral storytelling, fairy tales, nature stories
- **No academics before 7**: letters and numbers wait; the body and imagination develop first
- **Festivals**: seasonal celebrations and rituals mark the year
- **Handwork**: knitting, finger-knitting, sewing, weaving as cognitive work
- **The teacher / parent moves with reverence**: gentle voice, slow pace, intention

## Design implications for packets

- **Beautiful, simple design**: muted colors (esp. for under-7), watercolor feel
- **Storytelling-driven**: many activities open with a short imaginative story
- **Seasonal awareness**: weave in the current season, weather, festival
- **Natural-material craft callouts**: wool, beeswax, wood, plant materials
- **Less academic, more imaginative for under-7**: avoid heavy letter/number drills for younger Waldorf-aligned families (check `learningGoals` — if parents want letters, do letters; respect the framework's bias only when the family's hasn't overridden)
- **Rhythm prompts**: "morning verse", "candle-lighting", "song before"
- **Reverent, gentle tone**: parent-facing language is calm, slow, warm
- **Festivals woven in**: spring equinox, harvest, lantern-walks, etc., depending on the household's traditions

## Sample parent-footer line

> 🕯 **A Waldorf note**: light a candle, sing the morning song, then begin. Rhythm is the gift.

## Activity types that fit especially well

Storytelling · finger-knitting · watercolor (wet-on-wet style) · nature observation · seasonal crafts · songs and verses · candle-making · beeswax modeling · puppet-making · gnome / elf / fairy themes · wool felting

## What to avoid

- Cartoon character art
- Heavy plastic / synthetic materials
- Academic drills for under-7 (when the family is Waldorf-aligned and hasn't asked)
- Loud/bright primary colors for under-7 packets (soft, muted, watercolor feel)
- Screens-themed activities
- Speed framing
