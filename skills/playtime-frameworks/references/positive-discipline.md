# Positive Discipline (Jane Nelsen)

**Core idea**: Discipline = teaching, not punishing. Be **kind AND firm** at the same time. Build skills through encouragement, problem-solving, and connection — not rewards or punishments.

## Pillars

- **Kind AND firm**: warm relationship + clear limits, simultaneously
- **Mistakes are opportunities to learn**: not failures
- **Encouragement over praise**: specific noticing ("you kept going even when it was hard") vs. evaluative praise ("good job")
- **Belonging and significance**: kids' deepest needs; behavior makes sense as attempts to meet these
- **Family meetings**: regular structured family conversation for problem-solving
- **Logical consequences**: connected, respectful, reasonable, revealed in advance — not arbitrary punishment
- **Mistaken-goal chart**: behavior decoded — what's the kid trying to communicate? (attention, power, revenge, assumed inadequacy)
- **Positive time-out**: a regulating space, not a punitive one

## Design implications for packets

- **Encouragement language** throughout: specific, grounded, relational
- **Family-meeting prompts**: weekly or monthly card with conversation starters
- **Problem-solving prompts**: "what's something you'd like to figure out this week?"
- **Mistake-celebration moments**: a "today's beautiful oops" prompt where mistakes become art or insight
- **Reflection prompts**: "what did you do that helped someone today?"
- **No reward charts**: skip sticker-chart language entirely
- **No shame language**

## Sample parent-footer line

> 🌳 **A Positive Discipline note**: when you notice [Kid] persevering, try: *"you kept going even when that was hard."* Specific noticing > evaluative praise.

## Activity types that fit especially well

Family meeting prompts · feelings check-in · acts of kindness · concept-of-the-day (esp. virtues) · problem-solving prompts · gratitude journaling · pretend play with conflict scenarios

## What to avoid

- Reward charts / sticker bribes
- "Good job" / "bad job" framing
- Punishment metaphors
- Comparison to siblings or peers
