# The Whole-Brain Child (Dan Siegel & Tina Bryson)

**Core idea**: Integrate the developing brain — left/right, upstairs/downstairs — through how we talk to and connect with kids. Hard moments are opportunities to wire a more integrated mind.

## Pillars

- **Connect and redirect**: connect with the right brain (emotion) before reasoning with the left
- **Name it to tame it**: telling the story of a hard feeling reduces its grip
- **Engage, don't enrage**: appeal to the upstairs brain (planning, regulation) instead of triggering the downstairs (reactive)
- **Use it or lose it**: exercise the upstairs brain through choices, problem-solving, perspective-taking
- **Move it or lose it**: physical movement helps integration
- **SIFT**: sensations, images, feelings, thoughts — a way to introspect
- **Mindsight**: seeing one's own and others' inner worlds
- **The wheel of awareness**: a metaphor for noticing without being consumed
- **Remember to remember**: tell stories about the past to build coherent self-narrative

## Design implications for packets

- **Storytelling for integration**: prompts to retell a hard moment ("draw a comic of a tough moment this week and how it ended")
- **SIFT journaling**: occasional check-ins — what do you sense / picture / feel / think?
- **Movement integrated**: even cognitive activities include a body break
- **Name-it-to-tame-it scripts** in the parent footer for emotional moments
- **Coherent narrative prompts**: "tell me about when..." family-history prompts
- **Choice and agency**: kids decide a meaningful piece (color, theme, version)
- **Mindsight nudges**: empathy prompts ("how might [character] feel?")

## Sample parent-footer line

> 🧠 **A Whole-Brain note**: if [Kid] hits a wall mid-activity, try connecting to the feeling first. *"You wanted that to work and it didn't. That's frustrating."* Then, once they're regulated, problem-solve.

## Activity types that fit especially well

Comic strip of a real moment · feelings check-in · SIFT journaling · family interview · mindfulness · concept-of-the-day · pretend play with emotional content · gratitude journaling · drawing prompts about feelings

## What to avoid

- Pure top-down reasoning during emotional moments
- Failure framing
- Anything dismissive of feelings ("you're fine")
