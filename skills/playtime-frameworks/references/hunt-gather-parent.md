# Hunt, Gather, Parent (Michaeleen Doucleff)

**Core idea**: Modern Western parenting is one weird outlier in a long history of human cultures. Many indigenous cultures (Maya, Inuit, Hadzabe) raise calm, helpful, autonomous, emotionally regulated kids by doing the *opposite* of what we do.

## Pillars (TEAM)

- **T — Together**: kids belong to family work and life; "alongside" is the default mode
- **E — Encouragement** (vs. praise/coercion): kids are recruited into helping, not bribed; encouragement is real
- **A — Autonomy**: kids decide a LOT — when to eat, when to play, when to help — within a clear frame
- **M — Minimal interference**: parents step back massively; let conflict, frustration, boredom be what they are

## Companion principles

- **Acompañamiento**: literally "accompanying" — kids learn by being next to grown-ups doing real work
- **Less talking, more doing**: explain less; demonstrate more; keep instructions to a sentence
- **No fake play**: prefer real tasks (cooking, mending, building) over plastic play
- **Calm parents = calm kids**: regulating yourself is the work
- **Respect autonomy**: never coerce; if they don't want to, that's information, not defiance

## Design implications for packets

- **Real tasks > pretend ones.** Cooking, mending, plant-watering, household contribution
- **Apprenticeship framing**: "today you're the family's [chef/builder/scientist]"
- **Minimal instruction text.** Show with pictures; let the kid figure it out
- **Side-by-side activities**: parent and kid doing the same thing, not parent supervising kid
- **Encouragement language**: "you're a real helper to this family" not "good job!"
- **No coercion**: kid can opt out. The activity invites; it doesn't demand
- **Frustration is welcome**: don't engineer ease; let them struggle a bit

## Sample parent-footer line

> 🤝 **A Hunt-Gather note**: try doing this *alongside* [Kid] today, not over their shoulder. Sit, fold your own laundry, peel your own carrot. The magic is acompañamiento.

## Activity types that fit especially well

Cooking · practical-life invitations · how-tos (real skills) · gardening · mending · acts-of-kindness · family interview · contribution-as-craft (e.g. "today you make the family's bread")

## What to avoid

- Heavy verbal coaching during the activity
- Bribes / sticker charts
- Coercion ("you HAVE to finish")
- Pure-pretend play disconnected from real tasks
- Praise inflation ("amazing!" "incredible!")
