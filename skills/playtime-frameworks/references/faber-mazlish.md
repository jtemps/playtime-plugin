# How To Talk So Kids Will Listen (Faber & Mazlish / King)

**Core idea**: Communication tools — specific phrases, structures, and habits — change how kids hear us and how they cooperate. Less lecturing, more attuned, specific language.

## Pillars

- **Acknowledge feelings**: "you wish you could have it now" instead of "no, you can't"
- **Engage cooperation** with these tools (any one of):
  1. Describe ("I see toys on the floor")
  2. Give information ("toys belong in the bin")
  3. Say it in a word ("toys")
  4. Talk about your own feelings ("I get cranky when I trip on toys")
  5. Write a note ("Dear [Kid], please put me away. — Toys")
- **Replace praise with description**: "you wrote your name all by yourself" vs. "good job"
- **Free kids from roles**: avoid pigeonholing ("she's the shy one")
- **Help kids deal with feelings**: don't deny or fix — name and acknowledge
- **Engage cooperation with playfulness**: silly voices, pretend scenarios

## Design implications for packets

- **Descriptive feedback** modeled in instructions ("you'll have drawn 5 different animals by the end")
- **Acknowledge-feelings scripts** in parent footer for predictable hard moments
- **Note-writing prompts**: a printable note for the parent OR kid to write to each other
- **Playful framing** of instructions when cooperation is hard ("the puzzle is whispering...")
- **No "good job" language** in kid-facing or parent-facing copy
- **Identity-positive framing**: "you found a way" not "you're so smart"

## Sample parent-footer line

> 💬 **A Faber-Mazlish note**: instead of *"good job"*, try describing what you see: *"you tried three different solutions before that one worked."* Kids hear the difference.

## Activity types that fit especially well

Letter/note writing · feelings check-in · acts of kindness · concept-of-the-day · pretend play · family interview · gratitude prompts

## What to avoid

- "Good job" / evaluative praise
- Lectures
- Role labels ("artist", "scientist", "athlete" — fine if kid claims; not as parent-imposed identity)
- Denying feelings
