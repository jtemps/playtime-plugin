# RIE / Magda Gerber (Resources for Infant Educarers)

**Core idea**: Babies are whole, capable, intelligent people from day one. Treat them with respect: narrate care, slow down, observe before intervening, trust the child's developmental timeline.

## Pillars

- **Respect the baby**: as a full person, not a project
- **Narrate care**: tell baby what's happening before doing it ("I'm going to pick you up now")
- **Slow down**: caregiving is a relationship, not a task to optimize
- **Selective intervention**: observe; don't rush to fix
- **Uninterrupted self-directed play**: floor time without entertainment, in a safe space
- **Authentic emotions**: don't fake-cheerful; be real
- **Trust the developmental timeline**: don't prop a baby into sitting; don't help them walk before they walk; let them get there
- **Simple environment**: few toys, simple objects, lots of space

## Design implications for packets (0-3 yr only — RIE doesn't apply older)

- **Parent-facing format.** Packet is for the grown-up; baby is the audience
- **Narration scripts**: include the words to say *as you change a diaper / pick them up / move them*
- **Observation prompts**: "today, watch [Baby] for 10 minutes during floor time. Note what they reach for, what they study"
- **Simple invitations**: a basket, two scarves, a wooden spoon — not "stimulation"
- **No baby entertainment**: avoid things that "do the playing" for the baby
- **Real objects > plastic toys**: a measuring spoon, a wooden bowl, a silk
- **Authentic affect**: instructions to the parent are calm and real, not bouncy

## Sample parent-footer line

> 🌿 **A RIE note**: set up the invitation, then sit nearby and observe without intervening. Resist the urge to demonstrate. Trust [Baby]'s pace.

## Activity types that fit especially well (0-3 yr)

Treasure baskets · sensory invitations · narrated-care moments · uninterrupted floor time · observation journals · object-permanence games (peekaboo, hide-and-find)

## What to avoid

- Anything that interrupts self-directed play
- Cheerful "performance" affect from the parent
- Scripts that "fix" baby's frustration
- Lots of toys at once
- Plastic over wood/cloth/natural
- Walkers, jumpers, propping aids
