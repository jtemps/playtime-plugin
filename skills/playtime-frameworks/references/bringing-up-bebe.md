# Bringing Up Bébé (Pamela Druckerman)

**Core idea**: French parenting produces calm, polite, well-fed, autonomous kids — through a few culturally embedded practices Americans miss. Less hovering, more frame; less catering, more context; less praise, more genuine respect.

## Pillars

- **Le cadre (the frame)**: clear, non-negotiable boundaries (manners, mealtime, sleep) inside which the kid has total freedom
- **Le pause**: pause before responding to a baby's cry — sleep training adjacent; teaches self-soothing
- **Patience as a skill**: kids are explicitly taught to wait — no rushing in to entertain
- **C'est moi qui décide ("I'm the one who decides")**: parental authority is calm and unembarrassed
- **Food culture**: kids eat what adults eat; varied, real, respected; no separate kid menu
- **Autonomy expected**: kids do real things on their own from young; "Cours-toi" — figure it out
- **No ambient praise**: comments are specific and earned

## Design implications for packets

- **Patience-building moments built in.** "Look at this for 1 full minute before you start drawing." "Set a timer; sit and observe the bird feeder for 5 minutes."
- **Real food culture integration**: cooking activities are real food (a real soup, a real tart) not "kid food"
- **Boredom-tolerance prompts**: "today's invitation: 10 minutes of doing nothing on the couch with a notebook"
- **Crisp instructions, no over-coaching**: give the kid the activity and step back
- **Manners and graciousness woven in**: a thank-you-note prompt, a "set the table for the family" prompt
- **Real verbs**: "cook", "make", "build" — not "play"
- **Quiet moments**: solo focused time as a goal, not a problem

## Sample parent-footer line

> 🇫🇷 **A Bringing-Up-Bébé note**: try giving [Kid] the page and walking away. Resist hovering. They'll surprise you.

## Activity types that fit especially well

Cooking real recipes · setting the table · letter writing · sustained drawing · solo reading · observation prompts · sustained focus challenges · time-capsule prompts · acts of graciousness

## What to avoid

- Constant praise
- Over-explanation in instructions
- Kid-food framing
- Bribes
- Hovering scripts in parent footer
- Shielding from frustration
