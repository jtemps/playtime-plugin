# Reggio Emilia

**Core idea**: The child is a competent protagonist of their own learning, capable of long, deep projects expressed through "a hundred languages" (drawing, sculpting, building, dancing, dramatizing, writing, photographing). The environment is the third teacher.

## Pillars

- **The child as protagonist**: powerful, capable, curious, full of ideas
- **The hundred languages**: kids express understanding through many media, not just writing
- **Project-based learning**: deep, sustained inquiry on a topic — days, weeks, months
- **Documentation**: photos, drawings, transcripts of kid's words become a record of learning
- **The environment as third teacher**: thoughtful, beautiful spaces with provocations
- **The teacher as researcher**: curious co-investigator, not transmitter of facts
- **Emergent curriculum**: follow kids' questions; don't impose pre-set lessons
- **Community and relationship-driven**: family + school + community collaborate

## Design implications for packets

- **Project arcs across days**: same theme, deepening complexity
  - Mon: introduce theme via observation/wonder
  - Tue: explore in one "language" (drawing)
  - Wed: explore in another (building)
  - Thu: a question to investigate
  - Fri: a culminating expression (a "hundred-languages" mini-show)
- **Provocations** rather than directives: "what do you notice about water?" then materials to explore it
- **Documentation prompts**: "draw what you see", "what is your theory about why?"
- **Open-ended questions** in instructions
- **Multi-modal expression**: same topic via drawing, dictating, building, dramatizing
- **Kid's words honored**: "your idea was X — let's add it to the page"
- **Long-form, low-pressure**: not every activity has a finished output; some are for thinking

## Sample parent-footer line

> 🎨 **A Reggio note**: ask [Kid] *"what is your theory about this?"* Their answer is the activity. Write it down.

## Activity types that fit especially well

Long-form drawing · dictated stories · "hundred-languages" projects · nature observation · provocations and questions · dramatic play · model-building · project-based research · documentation prompts (photos drawn, theories captured)

## What to avoid

- Worksheets with right answers
- Heavy direction
- Praise as feedback ("good job!")
- Closed questions
- Cartoon characters / commercial themes (not banned, but soften them)
- Speed framing
